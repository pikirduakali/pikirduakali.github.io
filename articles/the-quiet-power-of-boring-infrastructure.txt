The Quiet Power of Boring Infrastructure
Tech | 28 jan 2026

Invisible systems are the only ones worth admiring.

Nobody claps when the power stays on. Nobody tweets about how the water pressure was perfect today. The things that work exactly as expected are the things we notice least.

I think good design works the same way.

The best POS system I ever used at a cafe was one I barely thought about. It just worked. The buttons were where I expected them. The modifier keys made sense. It didn't try to be clever or beautiful — it tried to be fast, and it succeeded.

Boring infrastructure is reliable. It's predictable. It doesn't surprise you.

There's a lesson there for how we build anything: websites, businesses, relationships. The flashy stuff gets attention. The boring stuff gets results.

I'll take boring over broken any day.
